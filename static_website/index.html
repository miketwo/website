About Me

Space and Education are my passions: the former from a drive to explore, the latter from a drive to share.

In terms of Space, I have spent the last 15 years in the industry, and have had a hand in regulating amateur rocket launches and designing, building, testing or flying 115 satellites. In all cases my goal has been to make space more accessible and affordable for everyone. I am proud of my contributions in this field and hope to continue contributing as I move forward in my career.

When it comes to Education, too many children, women especially, grow up thinking they're bad at math and science when they're not. This problem has existed for a long time, and is only recently receiving the attention it deserves. Dan Meyer and Ken Robinson gave my two favorite talks on this topic, but the short summary is that we're not teaching STEM correctly, and multitudes of brilliant children who can do science grow up thinking they can't. That has to change, and I want to help change it.
